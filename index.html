6 Desk Trading HeuristicsOperational rules for CEE & AT portfolio positioning under the energy shock
01
TTF is the primary risk thermometer — watch daily, act on weekly closes
Every sustained weekly close above €50/MWh adds a notch of inflation tail risk. Above €60: model CPI upside scenarios actively. Below €40: reassess shorts in industrials; CB hike premium unwinds. Use TTF front-month as the single best real-time macro indicator for this region. Source: EEX / Trading Economics.
02
Energy producers over-index vs industrials — the spread widens further
In every energy shock episode (2021, 2022, now) the long energy / short energy-intensive pair outperforms for at least 2–3 quarters. Current pair: Long OMV+PKN vs Short VOEST+LENZ. Spread is not yet at 2022 extremes. Historical ratio work supports further widening at current TTF trajectory.
03
Hungary is a conditional position — size explicitly for the 29 May binary
BUX YTD +7.4% looks attractive but hides a reflexive risk cascade. The S&P sovereign review on 29 May creates a binary: upgrade/affirm → relief rally; downgrade to BB+ → forced institutional selling of HGB and OTP, HUF weakening, −15–20% acute equity drawdown. Reduce gross HUF/BUX exposure to max 50% of normal 2 weeks prior. Long MOL/Richter is structurally independent of the downgrade.
04
Czech Republic is the regional safe haven — use PX as shock absorber
CNB has the best policy credibility in CEE. Nuclear baseload at CEZ insulates from TTF. Fiscal position is the strongest regionally (deficit ~3% GDP). Czech koruna (CZK) outperforms HUF/PLN in risk-off. Use PX overweight / CZK long as tactical hedge against CEE-wide energy shock. The 2027 CEZ upside catalyst (hedge rolloff) is a free call option if bought at current valuations.
05
Bank positions: rate sensitivity net of credit quality — not simply rate-sensitive longs
In previous energy shocks, the bank trade flipped: initially rates up = NIM up (positive), then loan quality deteriorates as corporates struggle with energy bills → NPL up → provisions up → net negative. The inflection typically comes 6–9 months post-shock. BAWAG/Erste look attractive on NIM today; model credit quality deterioration for Q3–Q4 2026. Poland's PKO/PEO face the same dynamic with added NBP hike headwind.
06
Storage fill season (Apr–Sep) is the next critical window — TTF is a calendar spread story
Europe must refill storage from ~25 bcm to ideally 80+ bcm by October. With Hormuz-routed LNG constrained, this requires either pipeline normalisation or demand destruction. The Apr–Sep TTF calendar spread is historically predictive of next winter's price action. Monitor weekly AGSI+ storage injections vs 5yr seasonal average. If injections lag by >15% vs seasonal norm in April, upgrade the Sustained Disruption scenario probability to 50%.
